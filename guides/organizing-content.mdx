As your knowledge base grows, good organization makes the difference between a brain that helps and one that overwhelms. Company Brain gives you several layers of structure — Spaces, templates, pinned items, and archiving — so you can build a knowledge hierarchy that scales with your team without becoming a maintenance burden. Start simple and add structure as you need it.

## Working with Spaces

Spaces are the top-level organizational unit in Company Brain. Think of them as sections of your knowledge base, each with their own members, permissions, and focus area — similar to channels in Slack or folders in a shared drive.

To create a new Space:

1. Go to **Settings → Spaces → New Space**.
2. Enter a name and an optional description. The description appears on the Space's homepage and helps new members understand what belongs there.
3. Add members by searching for teammates by name or email. You can assign each member a role: **Viewer**, **Editor**, or **Owner**.
4. Choose a color and icon to make the Space easy to identify in the sidebar.
5. Click **Create Space** to save.

Once created, the Space appears in the left sidebar and is ready to receive knowledge items. Members you added will see it in their sidebar automatically.

## Recommended space structure

There's no single right way to organize your Spaces, but a structure that mirrors your team or department layout tends to work well for most companies. The hierarchy below is a good starting point that you can adapt to your organization:

```text
Company Brain
├── Engineering
│   ├── Backend
│   └── Frontend
├── Marketing
├── HR & People
├── Product
└── Company-wide
```

The **Company-wide** Space is especially useful for content that every employee should know about — like the employee handbook, holiday calendars, and company-wide policies. Nest Spaces under a parent (e.g., **Backend** under **Engineering**) to keep related knowledge grouped without creating too many top-level Spaces.

## Using templates

Templates let you standardize the structure of common knowledge types, so every decision record looks like a decision record and every runbook follows the same format. This consistency makes items easier to write, review, and read — especially as your team grows.

To create a template:

1. Create or open a knowledge item that has the structure you want to reuse.
2. Click the **⋯ More options** menu in the top-right corner of the item.
3. Select **Save as Template** and give the template a name.

The template is now available to everyone on your team when they create a new item — just click **New Item** and choose a template from the template picker instead of starting from a blank page.

Here are four templates worth creating for most teams:

| Template name    | Key sections to include                                              |
|------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Decision Record  | Context, Decision, Alternatives considered, Consequences            |
| Meeting Notes    | Date, Attendees, Agenda, Discussion summary, Action items           |
| Runbook          | Purpose, Prerequisites, Steps, Rollback procedure, On-call contacts |
| Onboarding Guide | Welcome, Tools to set up, Key contacts, First-week checklist        |

## Pinning and featuring items

Some knowledge items are important enough that they should be immediately visible to anyone who opens a Space — think "Start here" guides, team charters, or critical runbooks. Pinned items appear at the top of the Space before the regular item list.

To pin an item to a Space:

1. Right-click the item anywhere it appears in the app.
2. Select **Pin to Space**.
3. If the item belongs to multiple Spaces, choose which Space to pin it to.

Pinned items are marked with a pin icon so it's obvious they've been intentionally surfaced. You can pin up to five items per Space. To unpin, right-click the item and select **Unpin from Space**.

## Archiving stale content

Knowledge has a shelf life. Policies change, projects end, and runbooks become outdated. Deleting stale content removes it permanently, but archiving is the safer choice — archived items are hidden from search results by default and removed from Space listings, but they remain accessible to anyone who needs to look something up from the past.

To archive an item:

1. Open the item and click **⋯ More options**.
2. Select **Archive Item** and confirm.

Archived items can be found at any time by opening the search bar and applying the **Archive** filter, or by visiting **Settings → Archive**. To restore an archived item, open it from the archive and click **Restore**.

Make a habit of reviewing and archiving stale content quarterly. You can filter items in a Space by **Last updated** to find items that haven't been touched in a while.

<Note>
  Only **Owners** and **Admins** can create new Spaces or change Space settings. **Editors** can create and edit knowledge items within any Space they're a member of, but cannot create new Spaces or manage Space membership.
</Note>
