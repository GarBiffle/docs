Company Brain's search goes beyond keyword matching — it understands the meaning of your question and finds relevant knowledge even when the exact words don't match. Whether you're looking for a policy document, a past decision, or a snippet buried in a connected integration, AI search surfaces what you need in seconds.

## How AI search works

When you type a query, Company Brain searches across every item and integration you've connected — documents, notes, messages, tickets, and more. Rather than looking for literal word matches, it uses AI-generated embeddings (vector representations of meaning) to understand what you're actually asking. This semantic search is combined with BM25 keyword ranking in a hybrid model, so you get the best of both worlds: conceptual relevance and precise keyword accuracy. The final results are re-ranked by relevance before being presented to you.

In plain terms: type a question in natural language, and Company Brain figures out what you mean — even if the source material uses different phrasing.

## Searching from anywhere

You can trigger search from anywhere in Company Brain without navigating away from your current page.

- Press **`Cmd+K`** (macOS) or **`Ctrl+K`** (Windows/Linux) to open the quick-search panel instantly.
- The search bar is also always visible in the **top navigation bar** — click it at any time to start a new search.

Both entry points search across the same unified index, so you'll get identical results regardless of where you start.

## AI-generated summaries

For complex or open-ended queries, Company Brain displays an **AI-generated summary** at the top of your results. This summary synthesizes information pulled from multiple relevant sources into a single, coherent answer — and every claim is linked back to the original item so you can verify the source.

Summaries appear automatically when Company Brain detects that your query is a question or requires information from more than one source. You can always scroll past the summary to browse the full ranked results list.

## Filtering results

The **filter panel** on the left side of the search results page lets you narrow results along several dimensions:

| Filter | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Space** | Show results only from one or more specific spaces |
| **Tag** | Restrict results to items carrying a particular tag |
| **Author** | Find items created or last edited by a specific person |
| **Date** | Filter by creation date, last-modified date, or a custom date range |
| **Source** | Limit results to a specific integration (e.g., Notion, Slack, Confluence) |

You can combine multiple filters at the same time. Active filters are shown as chips above the results list — click the **×** on any chip to remove that filter.

## Ask a question

Company Brain supports direct question answering. Instead of browsing a list of results, type your question exactly as you'd ask a colleague:

- "What is our vacation policy?"
- "How do we handle customer refunds?"
- "Who owns the infrastructure roadmap?"

Company Brain extracts a direct answer from your knowledge base and presents it at the top of the page, along with the specific items (and sections within them) it used to construct the answer. Every answer includes **source citations** so you can trace the information back to its origin.

If no confident answer is found, Company Brain falls back to showing the most relevant search results rather than guessing.

## Search history

The `Cmd/Ctrl+K` quick-search panel keeps a list of your **recent searches** so you can revisit past queries without retyping them. Your search history appears beneath the search input when the panel is open and no query has been typed yet. Click any entry in the list to re-run that search instantly.

Search history is personal — only you can see your own recent searches.

<Tip>
  Use natural language questions for the best results. For example, **"How do we handle customer refunds?"** will return more relevant results than a bare keyword like **"refund policy"** — because Company Brain understands intent, not just terms.
</Tip>
