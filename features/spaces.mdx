Spaces are how you organize Company Brain for different teams, projects, or topics — each space is a curated section of your knowledge base with its own members and access controls. Rather than one undifferentiated pile of company knowledge, spaces let you give every team or initiative its own dedicated home while keeping everything searchable and connected across the whole workspace.

## What is a space?

A space is a named collection of knowledge items with its own identity and audience. Each space has a **name**, **description**, **color**, and **icon** that make it recognizable at a glance in the sidebar and the knowledge graph. Membership is explicit — only people you add can see (or contribute to) a private space.

Use spaces to model the natural structure of your organization:

- **Teams** — Engineering, Marketing, HR, Sales, Finance
- **Projects** — Project Atlas, Q1 Product Launch, Website Redesign
- **Cross-cutting topics** — Onboarding, Security & Compliance, Company Policies

A single item can belong to multiple spaces, so knowledge that's relevant to more than one team is never siloed.

## Creating a space

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Space settings">
    Go to **Settings → Spaces** from the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new space">
    Click **New Space** in the top-right corner of the Spaces page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the space identity">
    Set the space's **name**, **description**, **color**, and **icon**. These are shown in the sidebar and in the knowledge graph legend.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add members and assign roles">
    Search for team members by name or email and assign each one a role (see [Space roles](#space-roles) below).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the space">
    Click **Create**. The space is immediately available in the sidebar for all members you've added.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Space roles

Roles control what members can do within a specific space, independently of their workspace-level role.

| Role | Permissions |
|------|-------------|
| **Space Owner** | Create, edit, and delete the space; manage members and all space settings |
| **Space Admin** | Manage space members, pin items, and change space settings (cannot delete the space) |
| **Editor** | Create and edit knowledge items within the space |
| **Viewer** | Read-only access to all items in the space |

Workspace Owners and Workspace Admins automatically have full access to every space — including private spaces — regardless of whether they've been explicitly added as members.

## Space home

Every space has a **home page** that serves as the entry point for anyone visiting that space. You can customize the space home with:

- A **pinned header** — a short welcome message or orientation note for new members.
- **Featured items** — pin the most important items (e.g., team handbook, quick-start guide) so they're always front and center.
- A **brief description** — a paragraph explaining the space's purpose and what kind of knowledge lives here.

To edit the space home, open the space and click **Edit Home** in the top-right corner. Changes are visible to all space members immediately.

## Cross-space items

Items in Company Brain are not locked to a single space. You can add any item to multiple spaces, and the same underlying item will appear in each space's item list. This means:

- **Edits propagate everywhere** — when you update an item, the change is reflected in every space it belongs to.
- **There's no duplication** — you're not copying the item, just making it visible in an additional context.
- **Search is unified** — a cross-space item appears in search results for any member who has access to at least one of its spaces.

To add an item to an additional space, open the item, click the **Spaces** field in the right-hand metadata panel, and select the spaces you want.

<Tip>
  Use spaces to manage sensitive permissions cleanly. If certain knowledge (like compensation bands or HR investigations) should only be visible to a specific team, create a **private space**, restrict membership to the relevant people, and store that knowledge there. Members outside the space won't see those items — even in search results.
</Tip>
