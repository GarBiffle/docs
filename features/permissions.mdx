Company Brain uses a two-level permission model — workspace-level roles control what members can do globally across your entire Company Brain account, and space-level roles control access within individual spaces. This lets you give team members broad access to the workspace while still restricting sensitive spaces to the right people.

## Workspace roles

Every member of your Company Brain workspace is assigned one of the following roles. These roles govern what they can do at the platform level, independent of any specific space.

| Role | Can do |
|------|--------|
| **Owner** | Everything: manage billing and subscription, delete the workspace, configure all settings, manage all members and spaces |
| **Admin** | Manage members, create and manage spaces, configure integrations, and adjust workspace settings — but cannot change billing or delete the workspace |
| **Editor** | Create and edit knowledge items in any space they belong to; cannot manage workspace settings or members |
| **Viewer** | Read-only access to all public spaces and any spaces they've been added to; cannot create or edit items |

A workspace can have multiple Owners. We recommend having at least two Owners so access isn't lost if one person leaves.

## Space roles

Space roles are assigned per-space and can differ from a member's workspace role. A person can be an Editor at the workspace level but a Viewer in a specific sensitive space, or a Viewer workspace-wide but a Space Owner for a space they manage.

| Role | Permissions |
|------|-------------|
| **Space Owner** | Full control over the space: manage members, change settings, pin items, and delete the space |
| **Space Admin** | Manage space members and settings; cannot delete the space |
| **Editor** | Create and edit knowledge items within this space |
| **Viewer** | Read-only access to items in this space |
| **No access** | Cannot see or access the space — used for private spaces to explicitly block non-members |

Workspace Owners and Admins automatically have full access to every space, including private ones, regardless of their space-level assignment.

## Item-level visibility

In addition to space roles, individual knowledge items have their own **visibility setting** that provides a third layer of control:

- **Public** — visible to all members of the workspace, regardless of which spaces the item belongs to.
- **Space** — visible only to members of the space(s) the item has been added to.
- **Private** — visible only to the item's owner. Use this for drafts or personal notes you're not ready to share.

You can change an item's visibility at any time from the metadata panel on the right side of the item view.

## Managing members

You manage workspace membership from **Settings → Members**. From this page you can invite new people, change existing members' roles, and remove members who should no longer have access.

### Inviting a new member

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Members settings">
    Go to **Settings → Members** from the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Invite Members">
    Click the **Invite Members** button in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the email address">
    Type the invitee's work email address. You can enter multiple addresses separated by commas to send bulk invitations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a workspace role">
    Select the workspace role you want to assign: Owner, Admin, Editor, or Viewer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invitation">
    Click **Send Invite**. The invitee will receive an email with a link to join your workspace. The invitation expires after 7 days.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To change an existing member's role, find them in the Members list and click the role badge next to their name to open the role selector. To remove a member, click the **⋯** menu next to their name and select **Remove from workspace**.

## Single Sign-On (SSO)

Company Brain supports **SAML 2.0 Single Sign-On**, letting your team authenticate using your existing identity provider (such as Okta, Microsoft Entra ID, or Google Workspace). SSO configuration is available in **Settings → Security → SSO**.

Company Brain also supports **SCIM provisioning** for automatic user lifecycle management. With SCIM enabled, your identity provider can automatically create, update, and deactivate Company Brain accounts when employees join or leave your organization — eliminating manual member management.

<Note>
  SSO and SCIM provisioning are available on the **Business** and **Enterprise** plans. If you're on a lower-tier plan and need SSO, contact [support@companybrain.com](mailto:support@companybrain.com) to discuss an upgrade.
</Note>

<Warning>
  Removing a member from the workspace **immediately and irrevocably revokes their access** to all spaces, items, and integrations. They will be signed out of any active sessions within seconds. Make sure you intend to remove the correct person before confirming — this action cannot be undone without re-inviting them.
</Warning>
