The knowledge graph is a live visual map of your company's collective intelligence — it shows how items relate to each other through shared tags, links, authors, and co-citations. Instead of navigating folders or running searches, you can explore your knowledge base the way ideas actually connect: as a web of relationships that reveals context you might never have found through a list view.

## Accessing the graph

To open the knowledge graph, click the **Graph** icon in the left sidebar, or press `G` from any page. The graph opens in a full-screen canvas view, giving you a complete picture of your connected knowledge. Press `Esc` or click the **×** in the top-right corner to return to your previous page.

## How relationships are built

Company Brain automatically analyzes your knowledge items and creates edges (connections) between them based on four relationship signals:

- **Shared tags** — two items that carry the same tag are connected.
- **Explicit links** — items linked to each other using `[[Item Name]]` notation are directly connected.
- **Search co-occurrence** — items that are frequently searched and opened together are connected as likely related topics.
- **Authorship proximity** — items created by the same author within a similar time window are loosely connected, surfacing thematic clusters in someone's work.

You don't need to configure any of this — the graph builds and refines itself automatically as your team adds and updates knowledge.

## Navigating the graph

The graph canvas gives you full control over how you explore your knowledge:

- **Zoom in/out** — scroll with your mouse wheel or use the `+` / `−` controls in the bottom-right corner of the canvas.
- **Pan** — click and drag on the canvas background to move around.
- **Open an item** — click any node to open an item preview panel on the right side of the screen. From the preview, click **Open** to navigate to the full item.
- **Node colors** — each node is colored according to the space it belongs to. Every space has a unique color, shown in the legend at the bottom-left of the canvas.
- **Node size** — larger nodes have more items linking to them, making it easy to spot the most central pieces of knowledge in your graph.

## Graph filters

The **filter toolbar** at the top of the graph canvas lets you focus on the part of the graph that matters most:

| Filter | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Space** | Show only nodes belonging to the selected space(s) |
| **Tag** | Show only nodes that carry the selected tag(s) |
| **Author** | Show only nodes created or owned by the selected person |
| **Date range** | Show only nodes created or modified within the selected period |

Applying a filter dims unrelated nodes and highlights the matching subgraph, making it easy to explore a focused slice of your knowledge without losing the surrounding context. Clear all filters to return to the full graph.

## Discovering related knowledge

You don't need to open the graph to benefit from its relationship data. Whenever you're reading a knowledge item, Company Brain displays a **Related Items** panel in the right sidebar showing the top 5 items most closely connected to the one you're viewing.

Click any item in the **Related Items** panel to navigate directly to it. This makes it easy to follow a thread of connected knowledge without leaving your reading flow.

## Cluster view

Switch to **Cluster view** using the toggle in the top-right of the graph canvas. In this view, the AI groups related nodes into named clusters — automatically discovered topic areas that reflect the natural structure of your knowledge base. Each cluster is displayed as a labeled bubble enclosing its member nodes.

Cluster view is especially useful when onboarding new team members or auditing your knowledge base — it gives you an immediate bird's-eye view of what topic areas exist and how large they are relative to each other.

<Note>
  The knowledge graph updates in real time. As soon as a new item is added, a link is created, or a tag is applied, the graph reflects the change — you never need to manually refresh or rebuild it.
</Note>
